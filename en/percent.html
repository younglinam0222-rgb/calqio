Percentage Calculator — Complete Guide

Percentage is the most-used concept in investing. Returns, fees, yields, discounts, and portfolio weights are all expressed as percentages.

4 Calculation Types

① What % is A of B? → A ÷ B × 100 ② What is N% of A? → A × N ÷ 100 ③ % Change? → (New - Old) ÷ Old × 100 ④ Value after N% change? → Original × (1 + N ÷ 100)

The Asymmetry Trap

A 50% drop requires a 100% gain to recover. A 33% drop requires a 50% gain. This asymmetry is why limiting losses is so critical to long-term investing success.
