Dividend Yield Calculator — Complete Guide

Dividend investing generates cash flow simply by holding stocks. Understanding yield and after-tax income is essential for evaluating dividend investments.

Dividend Yield Formula

Yield (%) = (Annual Dividend per Share ÷ Stock Price) × 100 After-Tax = Annual Dividend × (1 - Tax Rate)

US qualified dividends: 0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income bracket. Check your broker for foreign stock withholding rates.

Warning Signs

Yields above 8% often signal a falling stock or unsustainable payout. Check payout ratio — above 100% means the company pays more than it earns.

💡 Always check the payout ratio and earnings trend alongside the yield.
