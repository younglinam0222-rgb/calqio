Average Down Calculator — Complete Guide

Averaging down means buying more shares when the price drops to lower your average cost basis. A lower average means you need less of a recovery to break even.

Average Cost Formula

New Avg = (Existing Value + New Purchase) ÷ (Existing Qty + New Qty)

Example: Hold 100 shares at $50, price drops to $40. Buy 100 more → new average $45. Break-even drops from +25% to +12.5% needed.

  • Check fundamentals: Only average down if the company is still strong.
  • Scale in: Don't use all reserves at once — buy in stages.
💡 Always verify why the price dropped before buying more.
